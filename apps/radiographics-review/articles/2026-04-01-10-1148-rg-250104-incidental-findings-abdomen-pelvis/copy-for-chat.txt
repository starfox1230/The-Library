Update on Management of Incidental Findings Seen on Imaging Studies of the Abdomen and Pelvis

Core concept: Most abdominal and pelvic incidental findings are benign; management should reduce unnecessary cascades while still identifying lesions with meaningful malignant or obstructive risk.

Liver findings: Low-risk patients with small homogeneous hyperenhancing liver lesions at CT or classic hyperechoic hemangioma-like lesions at US often need no follow-up; high-risk context, heterogeneity, or larger size shifts management toward MRI.

Biliary findings: Asymptomatic biliary ductal dilatation without an obstructing cause or abnormal liver tests usually does not need additional follow-up, but suspicious gallbladder wall thickening or polyp morphology warrants targeted evaluation.

Renal findings: Homogeneous renal masses measuring 21-30 HU at portal venous phase CT are very likely benign cysts, whereas lesions above 30 HU, heterogeneous lesions, and fat-poor enhancing masses need further imaging.

Other high-yield rules: Short uncomplicated enteroenteric intussusceptions, low-risk misty mesentery without lymphadenopathy, and low-risk testicular microlithiasis generally do not require imaging follow-up.
