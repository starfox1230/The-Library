The Decibel Scale

Understanding Logarithmic Relativity

The Decibel (dB) scale compresses massive changes in physical intensity (like sound or light) into small, manageable numbers. It compares a final intensity ($I_2$) against an initial reference intensity ($I_1$).


  • +3 dB ≈ Double the intensity (2x).
  • +10 dB = Ten-fold increase (10x).
  • -10 dB = One-tenth the intensity (0.1x).
