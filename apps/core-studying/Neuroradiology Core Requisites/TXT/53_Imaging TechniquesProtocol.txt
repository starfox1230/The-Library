                                                                                  Axial T1 precontrast and postcontrast and short inversion
                                                                                    time inversion recovery (STIR) large field of view should be
                                                                                    performed to cover the neck from skull base to thoracic inlet
                                                                                    to evaluate nodes and the aerodigestive tract mucosa.

                                                                                  Ideally, the remainder of the exam should be tailored per
                                                                                    the organ subsite using small field of view for higher res-
                                                                                    olution (<3mm) and improved soft-tissue detail.

                                                                                  If perineural extension of disease at the skull base is
                                                                                    in question, thin section T1 and T2images in multiple
                                                                                    planes without and with contrast through the skull base
                                                                                    can be performed. Postcontrast T1 images can provide
                                                                                    excellent detail if good quality fat saturation is applied.

                                                                                  Ultra-high resolution T2-weighted (T2W) constructive
                                                                                    interference steady state (CISS) and T1-weighted (T1W)
                                                                                    pre- and postcontrast volumetric interpolated breath-
                                                                                    hold examination (VIBE) imaging can be used for prob-
                                                                                    lem solving.
