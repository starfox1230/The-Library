                                                                          Pulsation Artifact in Aneurysms on Magnetic Resonance Imaging

                                                                          · This artifact typically occurs in the phase-encoding
                                                                             direction on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), usually
                                                                             in the short axis on brain imaging, resulting in ghost-
                                                                             ing appearance of both normal and pathologic pulsing
                                                                             structures across the image.

                                                                          · Usually, the pulsing structure can be identified because
                                                                             the "ghost" shows similar morphologic appearance.

                                                                          · Pulsation artifact can be confirmed by switching the
                                                                             phase-encoding direction, which will cause the artifact
                                                                             to project in this new direction

                                                                          · Presaturation bands placed outside the field of view can
                                                                             help suppress the artifact, however, it is the presence of
                                                                             the artifact itself that helps cinch the diagnosis of
                                                                             aneurysms or other high-flow vascular malformations!
