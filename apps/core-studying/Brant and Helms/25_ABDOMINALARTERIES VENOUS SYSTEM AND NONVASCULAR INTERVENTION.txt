                                                                           Hepatic Angiography and Intervention

                                                                       The common hepatic artery arises from the celiac trunk. The
                                                                       gastroduodenal artery (GDA) is its first major branch, with
                                                                       the artery continuing as the proper hepatic artery. Branch
                                                                       arteries parallel the portal veins and supply the liver segments.
                                                                       In approximately 55% of people, the right, left, and middle
                                                                       hepatic artery arises from the common hepatic artery. The cys-
                                                                       tic artery often arises from the right posterior branch. The
                                                                       middle hepatic artery may arise from either the left or the right
                                                                       hepatic artery. It supplies liver segments IVa and IVb.
