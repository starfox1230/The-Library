Myocardial Infarction Imaging
Tc-99m bone scan radiopharmaceuticals are taken up by the heart in
the region of a recent myocardial infarction. Tc-99m pyrophosphate
has been used for this purpose because it has higher soft tissue uptake
than other Tc-99m diphosphonates. This imaging test is no longer
requested by cardiologists to confirm the diagnosis of infarction.
However, diffusely increased uptake may also be seen with
myocarditis, postradiation injury, doxorubicin cardiotoxicity, and one
form of amyloidosis, as discussed in the following subsection. After
cell death, an influx of calcium occurs. Calcium phosphate complexes
are formed, and these microcrystalline deposits act as sites for bone
tracer uptake. Binding also occurs on denatured macromolecules.
