Cardiovascular System
A number of noninvasive cardiac diagnostic imaging studies are
available to the cardiologist, which include echocardiography,
computed tomography (CT), CT angiography, and magnetic
resonance imaging. The continuing value of cardiac nuclear
scintigraphic studies is that they are noninvasive, contrast media is
not required, and they accurately portray a wide range of
physiological and metabolic parameters that predict prognosis and
risk. Single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and
single-photon emission computed tomography with computed
tomography (SPECT/CT) have become the predominant imaging
modalities (Fig. 16.1). Positron emission tomography with computed
tomography (PET/CT) is growing in clinical use, whereas multigated
acquisition (MUGA) has an important but reduced clinical role today.
