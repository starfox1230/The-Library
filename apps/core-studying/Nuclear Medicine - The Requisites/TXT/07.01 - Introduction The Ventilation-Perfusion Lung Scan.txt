The Pulmonary System
Introduction: The Ventilation–Perfusion Lung
Scan
Particles slightly larger than red blood cells can be radiolabeled and
injected into a peripheral vein. After passing through the heart and
central pulmonary arteries, they finally lodge in the peripheral lung
capillaries, creating a map of pulmonary blood flow that can be
imaged with a gamma camera. Similarly, inhalation of a radiolabeled
gas or aerosol can allow ventilation imaging. These ventilation (V) and
perfusion (Q) examinations are the two components that make up the
VQ lung scan (Figs. 7.1 and 7.2). Although the VQ scan is most
commonly performed to diagnose suspected pulmonary embolism, it
can also be used for other purposes, including quantitation of
pulmonary function, often pre– or post–lung resection or transplant
surgery, and assessment of corrective surgery on pulmonary
vasculature.
