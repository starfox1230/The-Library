Key Point - There is a threshold. Once you cross the threshold you get the result (burn, cataract, gut

Key Point- After you cross this threshold the more dose you get the more severe that thing gets.
Stochastic - This is the “ Shit happens" model. Why do I call it that? Because it's a no-threshold by
chance model. I low much radiation till you get cancer? A little or a lot - no one knows. BUT there
is a relationship with dose and chance. The more dose you get the more likely you will get cancer -

Another example of this would be "Big River Man" - How the fuck is that guy not dead? If you
aren’t familiar with him he is a big bellied Slovenian man who drives drunk , eats horse burgers, and
swims naked in the Amazon river (among other rivers). There is a netllix documentary on the dude.
This guy is filled with Amazonian parasites, and he's not dead. Meanwhile you have random bad
luck - trees falling on people etc., who arc very healthy and don't swim in the Amazon. Obviously his
behavior increases his risk - but there is no threshold - cause and effect death model.
Key Point - Stochastic Effects are Chance Based - and follow a Linear Quadratic No Threshold
model - where risk increases with dose but doesn't automatically result in a bad outcome.
