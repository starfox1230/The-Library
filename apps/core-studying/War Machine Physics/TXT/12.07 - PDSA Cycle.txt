Making People Miserable - PDSA Cycle - “ Improvement Cycle”
A potentially endless cycle of torment - imposed by the QA officer.
The idea behind a PDSA Cycle is to test quality improvement interventions.
A PQ1 project might be an example. Think about this like the scientific method. You want to
improve turn around time for the daytime oncology clinic cancer followup pan scans. You
decided to try and incentivi/.e the residents on the body CT service to pick up the pace by telling
them that if they can't make the metric that they will need “ Additional Training" i.e. more night
float.
You set up a metric, and let the residents try and make it for 3 months. After the 3 month mark
you study the results, and based on those results you decide if the plan worked or didn't work.
If it didn i work ? You modify it by making the punishment more severe-torture ? Yes. But not
of the body, of the soul.
If it did work ? You still aren't satisfied. You want perpetual improvement. So you set the bar
even higher and start the cycle of torture over again.
