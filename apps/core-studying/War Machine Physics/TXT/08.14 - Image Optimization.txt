Image Optimization
This vs That: Output Power vs Receiver Gain:
Output Power (“Transmit Gain) - Cranking this knob up increases the brightness
by adjusting the strength of the sound pulse SENT TO THE BODY by transducer.
When the image is too bright from high output power, this will degrade lateral
resolution (think about it widening the beam).
Receiver Gain - Cranking this knob up increases brightness by adjusting the
strength of the sound pulse AFTER IT RETURNS to the transducer. Think about
this like a post processing technique.
•BOTH CAN LIGHTEN or DARKEN THE ENTIRE IMAGE.
•If Forced to Choose ALARA favors Receiver Gain is a first option
TGC- Time Gain Compensation
This is the button you push to make the image look better.
What it actually does is progressively increase the amount of
amplification applied with depth. The man who lives in the
ultrasound machine (“computer” I call it), is trying to make the
top and bottom uniform. This used to require manual
manipulation of buttons, but it is now automated.
What can you do to make this image better? - Answer is probably hit the TGC
button.
What you need to know:
• Purpose is uniform brightness from top to bottom
• It works by compensating for loss of echo strength caused by the depth of the
reflector
• Treats echoes differently depending on which depth they are returning from
• You could manually manipulate it by wiggling buttons around (now automated
with a bottom push)
