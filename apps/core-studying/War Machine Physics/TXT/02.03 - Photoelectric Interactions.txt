Unlike Classical Interaction (Coherent) which occurs at low energies ( lOish), or Compton
scatter which occurs at higher energies. Photoelectric occurs throughout the diagnostic range

1'he situation is that your x-ray strikes an inner shell electron. For the P.E. interaction to
occur the energy of the x-ray must be equal (or greater) that the orbital shell binding energy.
You have to have enough juice to know it loose. 1'his interaction is strong enough to remove
it from the shell (orbit).
The incoming or “ incident" x-ray loses all it’s energy and is totally absorbed. This interaction
is referred to a "all or nothing” - either it can release that inner shell electron or it can't.
With this inner electron spot missing you get downward cascade and release of a
characteristic x-ray or you get the production of an Auger electron. Because Auger electron
production tends to dominate in biologic tissue (unlike Tungsten, a the target with a high Z)
you still end up with biologic damage from these free electrons. P.E. is still good because
this actually contributes to image contrast (Compton just makes noise).
