Compression
Mammography is performed in compression (another unique feature).
Why do they do that? There are lots of advantages. Mainly it makes for a more uniform
tissue - gives you less overlap, less geometric blurring, and better detail. You would have
a TON of call backs if you didn’t do it., even more than you do already.The most testable
advantage is probably the dose / scatter reduction.
Here is the full list of reasons:
Reduced Thickness = Less Scatter = Lower kVp can be chosen
Lower kVp and Less Scatter = Improved Contrast
Reduced Thickness = Less mAs needed = Less Dose
Breast Doesn’t Move = Less Motion Artifact
Breast Smashed Closer to Bucky = Less Geometric Magnification
Less Motion and Less Geometric Mag = Improved Spatial Resolution
