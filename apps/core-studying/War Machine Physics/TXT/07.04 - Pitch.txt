Pitch
Pitch is a super important (testable) concept. The big take home is that you have a choice; dose or
spatial resolution.If you overlap your scanning beams, you will have better spatial resolution but
more dose. If you uncoil the beams, your dose becomes less - but your spatial resolution suffers.
The concept is pretty easy and intuitive - but you need to know the vocab words to get the questions
right. So lets learn them.
Pitch: Pitch is defined as the distance the table moves during
the time of a single revolution of the tube divided by the beam
width. If the movement of the table is less than the beam width
you end up with a fracture (a number less than I). If the
movement of the table is move than the beam width you end up
with a whole number (a number more than I). If they arc equal
then obviously you get a 1.
• “ Pitch of 1” - A pitch of 1 - mean there is no overlap between slices.
• “ Pitch Greater than 1” - This means the table moved faster than the beam, and you have a gap
between your slices. The gap means your spatial resolution suffers,but you got less dose.
• “ Pitch Less than 1” - T his means the table moved slow, and your slices overlapped. This overlap
gets you increased spatial resolution, but your dose increases.
Pitch =
Table Movement
Beam Width
Pitch Greater than 1
- Table Moves Fast
- Creates a Gap
- Spatial Resolution is Decreased
- Dose is Also Decreased
Pitch Less than 1
- Table Moves Slow
- Creates an Overlap
- Spatial Resolution is Increased
- Dose is Also Increased
Example: Cardiac CT (where you really care about spatial resolution) is done with a pitch of 0.2
(very small).
Trivia: A pitch less than I is sometimes referred to as over-scanning "
