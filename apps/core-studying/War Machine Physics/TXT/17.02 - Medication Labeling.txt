Medications Labeling-
What gets Labeled? The rule on this is - before the procedure starts you are supposed to label
medicines. and 1 mean ALL the meds that are not labeled. This includes medicines in syringes,
cups and basins. If you know what’s good for you, you better label those fucking basins.
What if there is only one medication being used? You still have to label it.
Is there any exception to the rule? Yup- In the 2014 recommendations they say label stuff “ not
immediately administered” - implying things that are immediately administered don't need
labeled. They even go as far as defining this exception very clearly “ An immediately
administered medication is one that an authorized staff'member prepares or obtains, takes
directly to a patient, and administers to that patient without any break in the process” .
Where are you supposed to do the labeling? In the area where medicines and supplies are set up.
What do you have to write on the labels? You better write small - because they pretty much want
everything on there. Medication name. Strength, Quantity, Diluent and volume (if not apparent
from the container), Expiration date when not used within 24 hours. Expiration time when
expiration occurs in less than 24 hours
Exceptions to the label writing rules?
You don't have to put expiration date and time- ii you
arc doing a short procedure
What if the person doing the preparing / labeling is not the person doing the administering?
Then verification (both verbal and visual) is done by two people qualified to participate in the
procedure (the janitor doesn’t count).
