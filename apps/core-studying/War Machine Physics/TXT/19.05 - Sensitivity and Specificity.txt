I want to discourage you from using the A, B, C, D method for doing statical problems. This can
get you in hot water if the question writer flips the axis of the square (which they love to do). It's
just better to understand what you are actually measuring.
Sensitivity = This is the ability of the test to detect true disease. In other words, the amount of
true disease detected with the test / total people with the disease.
True Positive / True Positive + False Negatives.
Specificity = The is the ability of the test to detect people free of disease. In other words, the
amount of disease free people called negative by the test / total number of disease free people.
True Negative / True Negative + False Positives
A very specific test rules in disease. (SPIN ; SPecificity rules IN)
