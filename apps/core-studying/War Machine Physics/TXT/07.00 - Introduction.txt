Just like with mammo (and fluoro), the differences between regular diagnostic x-ray, and CT
lend themselves easily to multiple choice questions.
So what is different?
Most CT scanners today arc “ 3rd generation” - which means the x-ray tube and the detectors
spin around the patient in synchrony. CT X-ray tubes use tungsten alloy targets placed on high
speed rotating anodes. CT lubes are designed to operate at reasonable voltages (between 80
-140 kV) with very high tube currents up to 1000mA. CT scanners have both small (0.6mm)
and large (1.2mm) focal spots. Typically you need a large focal spot to handle all that power
100kW.
