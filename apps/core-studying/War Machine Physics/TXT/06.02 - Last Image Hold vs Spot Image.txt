This vs That - “ Last Image Hold” vs Spot Image
If you tell the computer to hold the last fluoro image you have on the screen that is
referred to as a “ last image hold” or a “ Fluoro Frame Shot.” This is different than a “ spot
image” which is basically a regular x-ray.
The key differences worth knowing are:
• Spot Image = Conventional X-Ray
• Spot Image = More Dose
• Spot Image = Large Focal Spot (because greater tube current is needed)
• Fluoro Image Shot = More Quantum Mottle (less photons)
• Both use a grid.
• Spatial Resolution of a Fluoro Image Shot = around 2 Line Pairs per mm
• Spatial Resolution of a Spot Image = around 3 Line Pairs per mm
• **Spatial Resolution is better with the Spot Image
Trivia: “ 5-10" digital spot films = the dose of 1 minute of fluoroscopy (assuming same
FOV).
