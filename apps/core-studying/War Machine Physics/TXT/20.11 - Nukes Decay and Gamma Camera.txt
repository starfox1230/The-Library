Instruments:
• Sodium Iodine Well Counter - basically a small gamma camera, with just one PMT. There is a hole
in the block of Nal crystal into which the sample is placed (so it’s surrounding the sample). If the
sample exceeds 5000 counts per second (a lot less than a micro curie), it will be under reported. It’s
good for "wipe test" samples.
• Thyroid Probe-This is a modified version of the Nal well counter. Dose is compared to a
calibrated capsule of the same radionuclide.
• Geiger -Muller ( Counter — this is a device used to detect small amounts of radioactive contamination.
I hc device has a gas filled chamber, which when it comes in contact with radiation becomes ionized
and creates some voltage between a cathode and an anode - lots of beeping and clicking occurs. “ Dead
time ? Although the device is very sensitive, it is also vulnerable to being overloaded by a large dose
of radiation. One Click and Stop = Dead Time.
• Ion Chamber -This is what is used when higher doses are expected.
