Pitch
• “ Pitch of 1” - A pitch of 1 - mean there is no overlap between slices.
• " Pitch Greater than /” - 'This means the table moved faster than the beam, and you have a gap
between your slices. The gap means your spatial resolution suffers, but you got less dose.
• " Pitch Less than l " - This means the table moved slow, and your slices overlapped. This
overlap gets you increased spatial resolution, but your dose increases.
• A pitch less than 1 is sometimes referred to as "over-scanning"
Hounsfield Unit
• HU increases by 10 HU, x-ray attenuation increases by 1%
• HU = 1000 x (attenuation of material - attenuation of water) / attenuation of water.
Window Width and Level:
• “ Level” is the midpoint of the gray scale display (the “ center” ). You want your level at the
attenuation of the thing you are interested in. For example, if you are interested in bone
you want a high level.
• “ Width” is selected based on what you are comparing. If you are comparing things with
very different densities you want a wide width. If you arc comparing things with very
similar densities (example white and gray matter), you want a very narrow window width.
Above the upper limit of the width everything will look white. Below the lower limit of the
width everything will look black.
Slices Width
• Single Slice = Beam Width (i.e. width of collimation)
• Multi Slice = Width of the individual detector
• *Obviously the slice thickness is then reconstructed to maximize signal to noise.
Beam Width
• Increased Beam Width = Reduced Scan l ime (larger coverage with 1 turn)
• Increased Beam Width = Reduced Motion Artifact (less scan time)
• Increased Beam Width = Increases Partial Volume (more divergent beam)
• Increased Beam Width Does NOT change the radiation dose (mAs in unchanged, even though
the scan time is less, a larger area of tissue is scanned at the same time).
Increased kVp
• Dose Goes Up: Unlike conventional radiography with automated exposure control, increasing
kVp in CT will increase radiation dose to the patient.
• Image noise will decrease.
• Iodinated contrast will be more conspicuous at lower kVp (such as 80 kVp), as the average
energy of the x-ray beam will be closer to the k-edge of iodine.
Iterative Reconstruction - “ Forwarded” information is compared to actual information and
differences are used to correct the image. What you need to know is (a) it can correct for
noise, so you can (b) use a lower dose. It requires some major computer power, that's why
older systems couldn’t use it.

Signal to Noise:
• Signal changes directly with increased x-ray flux. Twice the x-rays, Twice the Signal
• Noise changes by a factor of VN. In other words twice the x-rays, V2 or 1.4 the Noise.
Example 1: If mA goes from 200 -> 400 mA, SNR = 2/root 2 or 2/1.4 - so 40% increase in SNR
Example 2: If mA goes from 200 -> 800 mA, SNR = 4/root 4 or 4/2
- so double the SNR
