Dose
Lastly I want to point out the superior dose efficiency - given
( 1 ) The wider dynamic range, which would allow you to underexpose a little bit knowing that you can
correct it by windowing, and
(2)
You don’t have to depend on kVp so very much for contrast. With film kVp was super super
important for getting your contrast (remember small differences in Z = big differences in
photoelectric absorption). But with digital since you can adjust contrast and brightness afterwards
you can go up on the kVp (by 15%) and half your mA to decrease dose.
prat Version = Digital has the potential to have lower dose, relative to film because of post processing
options.
Gamesmanship = Another way to word this is “ Higher Dose Efficiency.” Thats a fancy way to say less
radiation.
