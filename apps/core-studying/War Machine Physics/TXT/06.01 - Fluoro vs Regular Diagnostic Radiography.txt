So what is different?
Regular diagnostic x-ray is performed with mA ranging between 200-800 and very short exposure times.
For fluoro to work it has to have very long exposure times. The trade off is, that if you want long
exposure times and you don't want to melt your focal spot (or the patient) you will need to decrease the
mA . Speaking of the focal spot - to limit geometric blurring a smaller focal spot is used.
