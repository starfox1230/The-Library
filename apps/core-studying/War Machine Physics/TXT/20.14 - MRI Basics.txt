MRI
• RF Pulse - basically a radio wave (not ionizing)
• Signal can only be measured when it is NOT in the longitudinal direction.
• T1 is defined as the time at which longitudinal magnetization is 63% of its final value.
• T2 representing the time at which the signal has decayed to 37% of its original value of
transverse magnetization (63% of it has decayed). T2 = related to spin - spin interactions.
• T2* = Tissue Spin Interaction + Field Inhomogeneity; "random + fixed causes”
• T2= Tissue Spin Interaction ; "random causes only”
• T2* decay is ALWAYS faster than T2
• T1 = Short TR, Short TE
• T2 = Long TR, Long TE
• PD = Long TR, Short TE

K Space
• The center of K-space is made from the information about gross form and tissue contrast.
• The periphery of K-Space is made up of information about spatial resolution.
Spatial Encoding
• Phase encoding is much longer than frequency encoding
Slice Thickness
• Thinner Sheer with Steep (Large) Slice Selection Gradient, and Thin Transmit Bandwidth
• Thicker Sheer with Shallow (Small) Slice Selection Gradient, and Thick Transmit Bandwidth
Table time
• TIME = TR x Phase Matrix x NEX
• Fast Spin Echo = Acquisition time is approximately proportional to 1/Echo Train Length
• 3D: TIME = TR x Phase Matrix x NEX x # Slices

• Small Voxel

Receiver Bandwidth vs Transmit Bandwidth
• Fat Receiver Bandwidth = Decrease SNR,
• Fat Transmit Bandwidth = Large Slice = Increased SNR
• Narrow Receiver Bandwidth = Increased SNR.
• Narrow Transmit Bandwidth = Thin Slice = Decreased SNR
