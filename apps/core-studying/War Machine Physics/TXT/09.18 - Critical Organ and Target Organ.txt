Critical Organ
I want to define two frequently used terms:
•
"Critical Organ ” - An organ that limits the dose of the radiopharmaceutical due to the
increased susceptibility of the critical organ for cancer. This may or may not be the “ largct
Organ.”
•
"Target Organ
This is the organ you want the tracer to accumulate in. It’s your organ of
interest.
A few generalizations on Critical Organs: If you are trying to figure it out, it's the organ
that the tracer is going to spend the most time in. For example Gallium ends up in the bowel.
Tc RBC scans arc going to end up passing through the heart a lot, unless they arc heat treated
then the spleen cats them. Tc-MAG3s and DTPA is going to be the bladder, but DMSA
(which sticks to the kidney) is going to be the kidney. For anything that uses Ircc Ic
(meckels scan), it’s going to be the thyroid. MIBG scans are going to hit the bladder (or
thyroid if it’s not blocked).
