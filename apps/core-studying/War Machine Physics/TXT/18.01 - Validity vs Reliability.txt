Validity vs Reliability This vs That
Validity = Docs it do what it is supposed to do? Does the tool measure what it's supposed to
measure? Does the simulation device actually prepare you for a real case / accurate mimic a real
patient?
Reliability = The degree to which the tool produces consistent / stable results. Example = you
step on the bathroom scale and it says you arc fat. You get off and step back on 10 seconds later
and is says “ Still fat.’' This is reliable.
Tools can be reliable but not valid and vice versa.

l ace validity = Does the doll have a human looking face? Or does the virtual colon thing look like the
real colon on endoscopy?
Construct Validity = Can it determine if you suck or not? Which should be the overall point of simulation.
In other words- does the test do what it says it does ?
There arc two subtypes of Construct Validity:
Discriminant Validity = Docs the simulation test have the ability to discriminate between things
that arc not supposed to be related. Classic example- a test that measures narcissism (a disorder
that plagues the high ranks of academic medicine) is not simply measuring self-esteem.
Convergent Validity- Does the simulation test have the ability to determine if two measurements
of things that should be related - arc actually related (the opposite of discriminant). Classic
example- a test that measures self-esteem can show that measures of similar constructs, such as,
confidence, and self-worth arc also related
Content Validity = Does it teach you what you need to know?
Concurrent Validity = Does the simulation test correlate with other similar performance tests
Predictive Validity = Does success in the sim lab = success in the real world
