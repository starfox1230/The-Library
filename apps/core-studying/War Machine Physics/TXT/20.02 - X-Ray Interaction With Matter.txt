Photoelectric Interaction
• Dominates at lower energy
• X-ray strikes an inner shell electron
• This interaction is referred to a “ all or nothing" - either it can release that inner shell
electron or it can't. With this inner electron spot missing you get downward cascade and
release of a characteristic x-ray or you get the production of an Auger electron.
• Probability of P.E. directly proportional to the atomic number cubed: Z3
• Probability of P.E. is inversely proportional to the energy cubed: 1/ Z3
K-Edge
• P.E. peaks right around the binding energy for that inner shell electron. That peak is called
the “ K-Edgc” and exploiting it is an important part of imaging
• Phis is taken advantage of by selecting a kVp between 65-90 when using contrast agents
like barium (K edge 37 keV) and iodine (K edge 33 keV).
• Remember 65-90 is selected - Not 37 or 33, Because the average energy is going to be
between 1/3 to 1/2 the maximum enemy - placing the k edge right in the meat of the band
- maximizing that spike in attenuation via the P.E.
