Informed Consent:
Who can obtain it?- You (the Physician) or if you are smart you can have a “ qualified assistant"- in
other words a Resident (in academics) or a PA/NP in private practice.
Who can give it ? Patient or a legal representative- they have to be the one listed as the go to in the
chart.
Ultimate Responsibility? Unfortunately it's you (the Physician performing the procedure)- if they
have additional questions etc... you are supposed to answer them.
Components of the Consent:
• What the procedure involves ?- We are going to put a tube through your grandmother's skin into
her stomach so she can cat.
• What is the benefit ? Although there is probably no longer term survival benefit given her
advanced Alzheimer's and other co-morbidities- BUT this will really decrease the guilt you have
about not visiting her in the nursing home. Now you can tell yourself you “ helped her"
• What are the risks? It will periodically get clogged and they will transfer her to the ED in the
middle of the night to unclog it/ give her IV fluids. They will probably call you and wake you up
in the middle of the night to come in and give consent for her etc...
• Reasonable Alternatives ? Let her die in dignity with hospice
• Risks of Refusing ? Your lack of understanding of medicine coupled with American culture will
make you feel very guilty for not “ helping her", and you may get accused of “ killing grandma” by
one of your drunken relatives at the next family barbecue.
