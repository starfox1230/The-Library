Ultrasound
• Reflection - Ultrasound energy gets reflected at a boundary between two tissues because of the
differences in the acoustic impedances of the two tissues.
• Refraction - Influences by (1) Speed Change- which is based on tissue compression, and (2)
the Angle of Incidence. “ Snells Law”
• Scattering - Smooth reflectors -Strength of reflection is highly angle dependent. Non-specular
(diffuse) reflectors Angle has no effect on strength
• I lighcr Frequency = More Scatter.
• I ligher Frequency = More Attenuation

Power Doppler:
• Look for the presence of flow without information on direction.
• Power Doppler docs NOT exhibit aliasing (both color and spectral can).
• Power Doppler has NO dependence on the Doppler Angle - you can measure doppler
totally perpendicular to the vessel.
• Power Doppler is extremely sensitive to flow (even very slow flow)
Aliasing Artifact
• This artifact occurs when the doppler shift is greater than a threshold called the “ Nyquist
frequency”
• Decreasing Doppler shift - by cither using a lower frequency transducer or using a
doppler angle closer to 90 (increasing the angle)
• Increasing the Pulse Repetition Frequency (which will increase your Nyquist) or selecting
a sample volume at a lesser depth or increasing the scale.
Image Optimization
• Output Power - what’s sent out
• Receiver Gain - what’s received
• Both can lighten / darken image. Adjust receiver gain first ALARA
• TCG - Makes the deep image look the same as the superficial image. “ Uniform
brightness”

Compound Imaging
• Compound imaging uses electronic steering of the ultrasound beams from the transducer to
image an object in multiple different directions.
• Will sharpen the edges
• Will cause loss of posterior shadowing
