DEXA Scan
Now is as good of a time as any to talk about the DEXA scan. DEXA or
Dual Energy X-Ray Absorption is a technique that relies on the
differences in transmission through different tissues at different energies.
Lets just look at it from an engineering standpoint - for the purpose of
understanding the principals, then I’ll talk about what I think is testable.
Basically you have two unknown quantities (1) Area of bone mineral, and
(2) Area of soft tissue. It’s easy enough to calculate how much a single
beam will be attenuated through the mass of both (you know what you
put in, and you can measure what you got out). But how do you tell
which was attenuated by bone, and which was attenuated by soft tissue?
There were some ancient methods that I’m not going to talk about
(submerging people in water, using gamma rays, etc..). The eventual
solution was to use two different energies- one high and one low. The
idea is that the bone is gonna block the low energy ones, but not the
high energy ones. The soft tissue is gonna let them both through. Then
you can do math to subtract out what is just bone. You make a bunch of
measurements- average them out and come up with a density (BMD).
Testable Trivia:
•
DEXA relies on transmission measurements made at two different
photon energies.
•
Two main methods for doing this:
o
Use a filter that drops the k-edge right in the middle of the
spectrum- essentially separating the curve into two peaks
- or two different energies (usually around 40 and 70).
o
Switch the tube voltage between low (70) and high (140) -
placing the effecting energy around 45 and 100.
•
Dose for a DEXA scan is VERY low - we are talking 0.001 mSv
(for perspective a spine x-ray is like 1.5 mSv)
