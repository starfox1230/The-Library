Radiologist Young and Old also hate “ Noise”. So what is this “ Noise” ?
Noise is basically data that contributes nothing useful to the image, and serves only as a
distraction. “ Quantum Noise ” is the more fancy term is the term / concern for digital
imaging. Think of it as background randomness. The piece of paper you arc drawling on is
not clean and white, it’s got some background smudges on it. If you draw with a thin pencil
you'll notice the background dirt a lot more than if you draw with a thick magic marker.
The key point to know is that this occurs as a function of the number of photons hitting the
imaging receptor.
• More photons (thick marker) = Less Noise
• Less Photons (thin pencil) = More Noise
T hat background variation (noise) is always there - you just drown it out with a heavier
exposure. Bottom line = anything that increases the number of photons on the imaging
receptor will cut down on the “ mottle” and therefore reduce noise.
“ Quantum Mottle” - A very popular term thrown around by people pretending to be smart /
know physics. Basic concept is that the x-ray beam is not perfectly uniform and at low
doses you notice it more. To use another analogy think about rain on a sidewalk. When it
first starts to rain the few drops on the sidewalk might appear random, but once the
downpour starts the sidewalk is saturated and you'd swear the rain is coming down in a
totally homogenous pattern (even though its not really - but almost).
Scatter - Remember the definition of “ noise” is data that does not contribute to the image.
So not all '‘noise” is quantum mottle (although that is the major contributor).
