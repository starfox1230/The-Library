X-Ray Production:
X Rays arc made by “ Thermionic Emissions of ElectronsThis is done by taking a filament
and running some current through it. The filament is typically made of Tungsten - because
Tungsten has a very high melting temperature (it can tolerate a lot of heat). Sometimes
rhenium (10%) is added to keep the Tungsten from cracking from heating and cooling. This
current is considered “ small” , but it still makes the Tungsten really hot (over 2000 degrees)
and therefore makes the electrons in the Tungsten atoms very energetic- until they “ boil off.”
The filament is a cathode (negatively charged). This repels the electrons as they boil off. They
fire down the vacuum chamber towards a positively charged anode (also usually made of
Tungsten).
The free electrons accelerate toward this target (the anode) because of the potential difference
between the cathode and anode. As they accelerate, they gain kinetic energy (kcV). When
the energetic electrons strike the tungsten target, they lose their kinetic energy via 3 different
methods (excitation, ionization, and radiative losses i.e. Brcmsstrahlung). X-rays are
produced as described on page 14.
