Rapid Review - Differences in Plain Film vs Digital
•Digital has “larger dynamic range” which means you can over or under expose the
film (within reason) and it’s still diagnostic. This means less repeats (so less
radiation overall).
•Digital has a linear response curve, Film has a curvilinear response curve.
•Film depends on kVp for contrast. Digital doesn’t as much - so you can use the
15% rule (increase your kVp by 15%, decrease your mA by 50%) and cut down on
dose.
•Spatial Resolution of Film may still be better than Digital
