Contrast Resolution
Contrast resolution can be defined as the ability to discriminate between structures with very
similar attenuation characteristics (CT numbers).
On the next page I’m going to talk about CT Spatial Resolution - and I 'll mention the fact that
CT Spatial Resolution is total shit compared to mammo (and even regular plain film imaging).
CT Contrast Resolution is a different story. CT Contrast Resolution is excellent - way way better
than plain film Think about it - with CT you can tell the difference between white matter and
grey matter in the brain. With plain film... it’s either bone, air,soft tissue, metal, etc...
Why is CT Contrast Resolution So Good?
Minimal Scatter Reaching the Detectors - This is accomplished via ( 1 ) very tight
collimation (both pre-patient and pre-detector) and (2) by windowing . A tight window
really maximizes your contrast resolution.
What is the limiting factor for contrast resolution ? Noise.
What will improve the contrast resolution? Essentially any factor that reduces the amount
or visibility of noise - so all the stuff I mentioned before (mA, kVp, slice thickness,
matrix size, FOV, and reconstruction filter).
