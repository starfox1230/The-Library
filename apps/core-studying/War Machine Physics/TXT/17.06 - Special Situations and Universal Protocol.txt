Special Situations:
When is consent not valid ?
(1 ) Patient has dementia or delirium (ean't understand the procedure, risks/benelits etc).
(2) Is a minor (and not pregnant)
What to do? Gotta get consent from an appointed representative
What about in emergencies? You can treat without consent to prevent serious disability or
death / or to alleviate great pain or suffering.
Universal Protocol Process:
(1 ) Pre-Procedure Verification
(2) Mark the Site
(3) Time Out
This is your checklist (manifest) to make sure you don't amputate the wrong leg.
Site Marking Trivia:
Person doing the procedure should do the marking (limited circumstances lor allowing
the NP/PA to do it)
It must be visible AFTER draping / prepping- in other words- near the site of the actual
procedure.
Time Out Trivia:
-
Should occur- IMMEDIATELY PRIOR to the procedure
Everyone in the room should agree (including anesthesia, nurses etc...)
-
Multiple Procedures = Multiple Time Outs , prior to each procedure (you can't do them
all at the beginning)
