Shewhart Charts (Control Chart)
1'his is a “ process-behavior” type of chart. This is best explained by thinking about the chart
as a tool to analyze turnaround time for ED studies. Yes there is variation. Some residents
arc faster than others. Some cases are more complicated than others. The time of the day
matters (I'm sleepy right after lunch). So that type of variation is expected - you would call
this "noise ” , Investigation of this would be a huge waste of time.
Spikes above or below this expected variation (above your upper
control limit, or lower control limit). These are considered
special. It’s the WTF happen there? Why did it take 6 hours to
turn around that ED study? That should be investigated. Maybe
the reading room coordinator put the study on the wrong list and
didn't notice because something really interesting was going on
with her facebook feed.
Upper and lower “ controls” are chosen to distinguish between normal variation (noise), and
special or unusually variation (signal).
Staying between the controls demonstrates a stable process.
This allows you to tell the
signal from the noise.

Variation
