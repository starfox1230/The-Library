What is this “ unsharpness ? ” For all intensive purposes “ unsharpness” means a loss of
spatial resolution. PhDs describe 3 subtypes of unsharpness
( 1 ) Motion Unsharpness - This is basically loss of spatial resolution that is attributable to
patient motion. A good way to ask questions about this would be to say “ how do you
decrease motion unsharpness” or address motion artifact? Good Answers would be
(a) To say something like “ Sir!! Hold Still!! Yes I know you’re leg is broken - but you
don’t see me screaming!” (b) Tie his ass down , (c) Decrease the exposure time. Less
“s” on the mAs.
(2) System Unsharpness - 1 prefer to blame someone anytime something goes wrong. If 1
was an academic 1 would blame the resident / fellow. Since I 'm not an academic 1
usually blame the tech or the nurse (for farts I usually blame the dog). In this case
system unsharpness is the fault of the detector. Every detector type has a limiting
factor which determines the maximum spatial resolution.
• Film = Size of the Grain of Photographic Chemical
• Computed Radiography (CR) = Size of the Laser used to read the phosphor plate
in the cassette reader
• Digital Radiography (DR ) = Size of the individual thermoluminescent transistor.
