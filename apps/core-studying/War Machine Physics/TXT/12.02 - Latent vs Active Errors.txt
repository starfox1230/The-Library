Stirring Up Trouble - This vs That: - Latent vs Active Errors
As 1 mentioned previously, one of the primary tasks of the QA Officer is Stirring Up Trouble.
1 le/She does that in a variety of ways one of which is pointing out people’s mistakes and then
criticizing them for it. Let’s discuss the lingo.
Latent Error - Conditions in the system which allow the error to occur
Example 1: I lave you ever pulled up to a stop light, and you went a little too far into the
intersection so you put the car in reverse and back up a little bit so you don't get hit? But then a
song comes on the radio that you really like and you forget to put the car back in drive- so you
arc sitting there waiting for the light to turn green with the car in reverse. You are truly an
accident waiting to happen. This is a latent error. Conditions in the system that allow the error to
occur, i.e. Justin Bieber on the radio distracted you -so you forgot to put the car back in drive. If
you didn’t have music distracting you - this wouldn’t have happen
Example 2: Sharps box is bolted to the wall away from the procedure area, so you have to walk
the needles over to it in a crowded room.
Why the fuck is the box on the other side of the room?
System Failure = Accident Waiting to happen
Example 3: Shitty HMR allows you to accidentally enter lethal doses of pain medicine.
Active Error: A mistake that immediately causes an injury. You did the fucking up. It’s tough
to blame the system - although you can still try and blame the resident.
Example I : Administration of the Wrong dose of medication
Example 2: Cutting the hepatic artery while trying to remove the gallbladder.
