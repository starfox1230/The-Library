Gamesmanship Summary:
If the question is - "which of the following will reduce noise ?" All of the
following would be correct:
•Use a Grid
•Use an AirGap (this works like a grid — it’s mostly a mammo thing)
•Increase the mA
If all the choices seem correct and you have to pick just one - I’d pick
increase the mA. How could that happen? If your text writer is using noise
and mottle (photon starvation) interchangeable. Really noise should include
scatter - but there is no guarantee that the people writing the questions
actually understand physics.
Be weary of (1) Questions about field size / coilimation questions, because
that mixes the results. Coilimation reduces scatter, but it also reduces
photons hitting the target. Typically there is an automatic increase in mA with
coilimation so your signal to noise ratio improves - that’s why coilimation is
good (plus the reduced dose). But there is the potential for mind reading /
fuckery - because if you don’t increase the mA although you may reduce
scatter the overall SNR may worse secondary to mottle. Just be careful -
read the wording of the question carefully.
Be weary of (2) Any answer choices asking you to reduce noise with post
processing - unless you thinks it’s talking about iterative reconstruction
technique - in which case they better say it. Just be careful, it seems like
this could be a trap. "It’s a trap!" - Admiral Gial Ackbar
If the question mentions a grid - and you suspect a "grid cut off" - the next
step is to reposition the grid. Don’t immediately jump to increasing the mA.
Remember most grid cut offs are from incorrect placement of the grid.
"Slap the tech around" - would also be a good option - if that’s available. It’s
second order, assuming you know the tech put the grid on wrong.
If the question mentions increasing (or decreasing) the distance between
source and detector, remember this relationship is not 1:1 - it’s governed by
the inverse square law - as described on the prior page.
