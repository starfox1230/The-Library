Once upon a time I was an intern- and trying to figure out what medications patients were on
during an admission I I&P made me want to stick my head in a dirty toilet (just to experience
something less irritating). “ I take a pink pill, and a blue pill
”
Medication reconciliation is awful and it’s one of the reasons people who aren't interested in
dedicating their lives to poop, pus, and note writing choose radiology over internal medicine, to
avoid these protracted horrible conversations.
The idea is- these people are on tons of meds, of which they may or may not even be taking.
You arc supposed to compare the meds they should be on (and actually using) to new
medications that arc ordered and resolve any discrepancies.
Testable Point - If the patient is unwilling or unable to provide you an accurate list a “good
faith" effort is enough to meet the guideline requirement.
Testable Point- You are supposed to provide the patient with written information on the
medications they should be taking when they are discharged.
Testable Point - Medication Reconciliation is supposed to be done at (a) Admission, (b) Transfer,
and (c) Discharge.
