Skill-Rule-Knowledge Classification Regarding Human Error
This system describes a thing called “ cognitive mode.”
It's sorta of a spectrum:
Knowledge
Rules
Skill
“ Skill” is the guy who tics your shoes for you. If you have every attempted to teach someone
how to tie their shoes you will realize how complicated it is. Lots of little steps- making a loop,
pinch, hold, pull, and its gotta be just right. When you were a little kid this took massive
concentration to do. Now you totally take it for granted and you can tie your shoes without even
looking down while having a conversation about something else. It's almost like someone lives
in side of you, and just takes over with little tasks like this. It’s the same guy who drives you to
work in the morning when you are tired and not paying attention.
Where docs that guy come
from??? Lots of reps. After you tied your shoe 10,000 times you started being able to do it
without paying attention. It’s on auto pilot. Practice doesn’t make perfect, it makes “ permanent.”
This is the “ skill” cognitive mode.
** Skill Related Errors are Slips / Lapses- You call your one kid the other kids name. Or much
worse you call your wife your girlfriends name... oops. In Radiology a good example would be a
right/left error.
“ Knowledge” This is the opposite side of the spectrum. T his is when you first learned to tie
your shoe. You know the moves but you have to really think about it. Every step is like you are
performing brain surgery and the slightest distraction can result in a catastrophic failure and the
need to re-start the knot.
*** Knowledge Related Errors are Mistakes- T his is when you fuck up because you don't know
what the hell you are doing. Usually preceded by the phrase “ how hard can it be?”
“ Rules” - This is somewhere in the middle. You operate with formulas for decision making
(sorta like the ER). If X then Y. Patient has “ Trauma” . Patient gets “ Pan Scanned. ”
