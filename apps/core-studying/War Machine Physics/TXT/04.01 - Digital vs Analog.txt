Digital vs Analog
Long ago, when dinosaurs roamed the earth, radiology was done on film screen. Now in the
modern age it is digital. 1'he comparison of film screen vs digital radiology could lend itself
to multiple choice questions.
“ Larger Dynamic Range”
One of the major limitations of film is it's limited dynamic range. What that means is that there
is a limit to the range of exposure intensities that it can accurately detect. In other words - at the
extreme ends (not enough and too much) film screen sucks, leaving it very sensitive to under or
over exposure. The consequence of that is more repeat studies (more radiation for the patient). A
second consequence of that is for situations when you want to look at two structures that differ
greatly in x-ray attenuation (example - bones and soft tissue) - film doesn't optimize this as well.
PhDs love to diagram this comparison of the dynamic range - this vs that, and I'm a firm believer
that comparative radiology makes for good test questions, soI think it’s worth being familiar
with the graph.
Testable points:
• Digital = Larger Dynamic Range +
Linear Response to Exposure
• Film = Narrow Dynamic Range +
Curvilinear Response to Exposure
