Stirring Up Trouble - This vs That: Blunt End vs Sharp End:
Blunt End = Think about the blunt end as the part you are holding - not the part doing the actual
damage. It's the tool you are wielding. Latent error occurs at the "blunt end ." The blunt end
might be the EMR which allows for a medication to dosed incorrectly.
Sharp End = Think about the sharp end as the part actually doing the damage. Active Error
occurs at the "sharp end." 'Think about this as the surgeon holding the “ sharp end" of the scalpel.
Ile is the active user / operator. I le is the one making the error. Another example would be the
nurse who actually gave the wrong medication.
Trivia: The sharp end is the “ last line of defense" against an error.
