Automatic Exposure Control - (AEC)
I tonestly, I probably should have described this thing already - but I might need to mention it
later in the chapter so I'll go head and do it now. During the Cretaceous Period the exposure
timing was done with an actual timer. Now a thing called the automatic exposure control (AEC)
is used. It essentially serves the same role as a timer but operates differently depending on the
patient body part - to determine the ideal amount of exposure. The underlying mechanism is
actually an ionization chamber - which I’ll talk about again in the nukes chapter. Basically when
x-rays interact with the atoms within the chamber they ionize them (through the same
mechanisms discussed earlier in the book). These ionized electrons arc now charged which
creates a signal to terminate the exposure. The AEC is calibrated to know how much of a charge
is good enough to terminate exposure.
Things to know:
• AEC acts like a timer which decided when to terminate exposure
• AEC works by using an ionization chamber, placed between the patient and the image
receptor
• The duration of the exposure is determined by the density and thickness of the area of the
patient placed over the ionization chamber.
• AEC controls only the quantity of radiation reaching the image receptor - no effect on kVp
(Fluoro Automatic Brightness systems - cun mess with kVp)
• AEC must be re-calibrated if that receptor changes (you upgrade from film to a CR digital
system)
