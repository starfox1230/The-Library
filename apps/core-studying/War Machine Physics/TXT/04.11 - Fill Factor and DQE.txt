Fill Factor
What is this “ Fill Factor" ? This is the area of the detector which is sensitive to x-rays (in
relation to the entire detector area). The higher the fill factor, the more efficient the detector.
Fill Factor Differences:
With DR systems, the electric field shaping essentially allows for a fill factor of nearly 100%.
This is not seen with Indirect (CR) systems. Remember, differences between things make
good multiple choice questions.

Detective Quantum Efficiency (DQE) -
Back on page 48. I went on and on about this confusing topic. As a rapid recap DQE is
essentially an estimate of the required exposure level that will be necessary to create an
optimal image. In other words, it's an efficiency measurement. The better your DQE the less
radiation you need to maintain your signal (High DQE = Low Dose). Another important
point is that your DQE struggles as your demand for spatial resolution increases.
As I mentioned above DR systems have a better fill factor (near 100%).

more efficient. So you could correctly infer that DR has a higher DQE.
