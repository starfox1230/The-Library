Spatial Resolution Concepts Related to Digital Imaging: I’m going to talk all about the digital
imaging receptors in the next chapter,but for completing the discussion of spatial resolution I
want to touch on a few points regarding the hardware. I’ll rehash this again in the next chapter
so if It doesn't make sense you don’t need to spaz it will all make sense by the time you finish
the book.
Even though film is ancient technology relative to digital imaging one of the few (probably only)
advantage it sort of has is in spatial resolution.
Film has the advantage of being composed of a continuous thing, where as digital receptors have
discrete information in the form of pixels. Now these pixels are really fucking small (they are
measured in microns),but they are still separate things. I'll touch on this again later but the pixel
size is a function of the matrix (more pixels in a matrix - the smaller they are). The more pixels
you have per unit area “ pixel density” the better your spatial resolution. Another vocabulary
word worth knowing is “pixel pitch” - which is the measurement from the center of one pixel to
the next (more easily understood as the pixel spacing).
• Testable Point: A major determinant of spatial resolution of digital images is the pixel size
and spacing (pixel pitch).
• Testable Point: Increased Pixel Density = Better Spatial Resolution
• Testable Point: Decreased Pixel Pitch = Better Spatial Resolution
