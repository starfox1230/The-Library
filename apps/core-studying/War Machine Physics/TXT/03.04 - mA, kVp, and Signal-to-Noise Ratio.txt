Signal to Noise Ratio (SNR)
This is a quantitative method for comparing the signal (stuff that contributes to
the image) vs the noise (stuff that doesn’t contribute to the image). If your
signal is really strong - you can tolerate more noise. If your signal is weak -
even a little bit of noise can wreck your image. Sometimes the term “contrast to
noise ratio" is used with a similar meaning.
This is how people typically talk about noise. If you say I’m improving/reducing
the "noise” - you are really improving the SNR. It’s a ratio. Things like higher
kVp may increase scatter - but do also improve overall SNR. I’ll talk more about
this in later chapters.
