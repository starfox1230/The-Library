Spatial Resolution — “ Pixels”
Film radiographs wcrc/are actually made up of tiny deposits of black metallic silver (imported from the
Byzantine Empire), smeared on as a single blob like unit. This is different than a digital image which is
recorded in individual units called “ pixels" as a“ matrix." Unfortunately, no one can be told what the
matrix is... you have to sec it for yourself.
OK fine.The matrix is a combination of very small (usually) rows and columns forming square, “ picture
elements."These picture elements arc your " pixels" . Think of each one of these pixels as a recorded
numerical value, which is going to be used to represent a brightness level on the display. The matrix size
varies - so the amount of pixels you have varies. For example a 1024 matrix has 1 ,048,576 individual
pixels (1024 x 1024).
So as I mentioned previously (page 49) pixel size is a function of the matrix (more pixels in a matrix - the
smaller they arc). The more pixels you have per unit area “ pixel density" the better your spatial
resolution . Another vocabulary word worth knowing is “ pixel pitch" - which is the measurement from the
center of one pixel to the next (more easily understood as the pixel spacing).
• Testable Point: A major determinant of spatial resolution of digital images is the pixel size and spacing
(pixel pitch).
• Testable Point: Increased Pixel Density = Better Spatial Resolution
• Testable Point: Decreased Pixel Pitch = Better Spatial Resolution
• Testable Point: Plain Film may have better spatial resolution than digital , because they don't have the
limitation of individual units (pixels).
