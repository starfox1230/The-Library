Contrast Induced Nephropathy (CIN):
Define CIN?
The stone age (“ historic") criteria is an absolute increase in the serum creatinine from by 0.5 mg/
dL. There are more modern definitions that require an absolute increase of up to 2.0 mg/dL, or a
percentage of change in the baseline serum creatinine of 25 to 50 percent- depending on the
study. The reality is CIN is way way way over diagnosed, and there are at least 2 large studies
showing that most things that are called “ CIN" are just normal variation in Cr that occur in
patients who arc sick in the hospital.
How often does CIN occur? Real answer is almost never. If asked on the exam, I would say
something like less than 10% even with moderate kidney disease.
When does CIN occur? The typical course is a spike in Cr within 24 hours of getting contrast,
peaking around day 4, then returning to baseline at day 10.
