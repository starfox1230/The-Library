Harmonics
As the ultrasound beam travels into the patient’s tissues the transmitted
pulse is progressively distorted. However this occurs primarily in the
central zone of the main beam where the intensity is high. A distorted
pulse will give rise to distorted echoes and these have significant energy
at harmonic frequencies. Tissue harmonics are made using the second
harmonic component of the echo signal, with the fundamental frequency
excluded. Undistorted echoes coming from lower intensity areas (fringes
of the beam, side lobes, superficial tissues) are not seen.
What you need to know:
• Harmonics involves transmitting at one frequency and receiving at
another - “the second harmonic” they call it.
• This is possible because body tissues cause some distortion of the
wave.
• The wave has to travel some distance to become distorted enough to
generate harmonics
• Harmonics are NOT produces in the near field (they haven’t traveled
far enough)
• This process IMPROVES lateral resolution
• There is a reduction in artifacts - specifically reverberation
• Because you are dealing with a higher frequency you do lose some
depth penetration (remember higher frequency attenuates- is
attenuated- faster).
