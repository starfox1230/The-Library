Lets do a quick run down on some vocab. I already introduced this idea of a matrix, and a pixel.
So what is this thing called “ bit depth” ?
Bit Depth -
Pixels have what is called "hit depth.” which is
basically the number of bits that determine the
number of shades of gray that can be displayed
on a computer monitor. For example a 12-bit
depth can display 4096 shades of gray, whereas
a 14- and 16-bit depth can display 16,384 and
65,536 shades of gray, respectively.
Where do those numbers come from ? Its
basically 2 to the “ x” power. So 212 = 4096.
Take-home point: The greater the pixel bit depth
(the bigger the number) the more shades of gray
available for image display. The more shades of
gray available to display on a digital image the
better the contrast resolution.
