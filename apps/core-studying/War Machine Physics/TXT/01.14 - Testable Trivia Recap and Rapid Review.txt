Testable Trivia Recap and Rapid Review
•Electrons from a heated filament will be pushed / pulled towards a target. When
they strike the target 3 basic things happen: (1) Heat Production, (2)
Characteristic Interactions, and (3) Brems Interactions
• “Characteristic Interactions” = Occur when an inner electron is forced out, and
it’s spot is filled by an outer electron. The exchange results in release of potential
energy in the form of a "characteristic photon.” This makes the spiky thing on
the spectrum diagrams.
•The energy of the characteristic photon is specific to the shell and the target.
The classic 69.5 K shell Tungsten.
•Brems interactions occur from “Breaking” or “Slowing Down” of the electrons
around a target nucleus. It occurs in a spectrum - with different energy photons
given off depending on high close the electron is to the nucleus (how much
slowing down it does). This makes the fat mountain looking part on the
spectrum diagrams.
•The average Brems energy is equal to one third of the kVp selected. The peak
energy is the kVp.
•Quantity (total number of x-rays) is directly proportional to mAs. Double =
Double. Quantity varies as the approximate square of the change in kVp.
Double = Increased Quantity by Four. Quantity decreases with distance - via the
inverse square law (change as the inverse of the square of the distance).
•Quality (penetrating power) is affected by (1) kVp -directly, and (2) Filtration.
Quality is measuring in “half value thickness” hence penetrating power.
• FIVE Factors will change the x-ray spectrum
•(1) mA = Beam Quantity
•(2) kVp = Beam Quality (and quantity)
•(3) Filtration = Beam Quality (and quantity)
•(4) Target Material = Changes Characteristic Peaks. Also increasing the
atomic number of the target will increase x-ray quantity * one of the reasons
tungsten is used. Also the quality is changed or "shifted to the right”
•(5) Generator Type = Improving the efficiency of the generator increases the
x-ray beam quantity and quality.
