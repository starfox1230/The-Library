Stirring Up Trouble - Benchmarking
What is this Benchmarking? Basically how well you are doing something relative to a standard
measurement or comparison to your peers.
The process to perform yourself some benchmarking is as follows:
( 1 ) Turn the thing into something that can be measured objectively. Lor example, patient
satisfaction. Mr. Metastatic Cancer Patient did you enjoy laying in the MR scanner for the
last hour? Can you rate the experience on a scale of 1-10? I lerc are some smiley / frowning
faces to help you decide what number to pick.
(2) Once you have an objective measurement then you can compare it to a national standard or
another institution. At our hospital the average patient satisfaction score for oncology
patients undergoing total spine MR is a 3/10. At rival hospital the same patient's give an
average score of 4/10.
The goals of the process is to look for ways to improve. What are they doing better than us? Well
they give their patients a lollipop and a sticker after their agonizing MR experience. Perhaps we
should try that too.
