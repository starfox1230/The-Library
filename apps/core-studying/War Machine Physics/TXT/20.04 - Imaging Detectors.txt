Digital vs Analog
• Digital has this larger dynamic range (compared to plain film)
• Larger dynamic range” which means you can over or under expose the him (within reason)
and it’s still diagnostic. This means less repeats (so less radiation overall).
• Digital has a linear response curve. Film has a curvilinear response curve.
• Film depends on kVp for contrast. Digital doesn’t as much - so you can use the 15% rule
(increase your kVp by 15%, decrease your mA by 50%) and cut down on dose.
• Spatial Resolution of Film may still be better than Digital
• Automatic Exposure Control (AEC) - acts like a timer for optimal exposure , it's mainly
messing with mAs
• Matrix: Images are displayed as a combination of columns and rows — “ array.” A bigger
matrix improves spatial resolution.
• Pixel: The smallest component of the matrix. A greater number of smaller pixels improves
spatial resolution.
• Pixel Bit Depth: Number of bits that determine the number of shades of gray that can be
displayed on a computer monitor. Can be thought of as a precision component controlling the
exact pixel brightness that can be specified.
CR & DR Rapid Summary
Storage Phosphors (CR): Can work with a conventional x-ray machine (just like a normal cassette).
The system uses a special phosphor (Barium Fluorohalides) that does not emit the absorbed energy as
light alter interaction with an x-ray. Instead, the x-ray causes an electron in the phosphor to change to
a metastable state (one that it can hold for several days).
• Readout Laser Spot Size: Smaller = Better
• Phosphor Pate Density / Thickness: More Thick = More Light Spreading = Less Resolution
• Sampling f requency (Rate of Light Sampling): Increasing the sampling frequency results in a smaller
pixel pitch with improves the spatial resolution.
• Imaging Plate: You are supposed to use the SMALLEST plate size reasonable for the anatomic area of
interest. The reason is that for fixed matrix size CR systems using a smaller plate for a given field of
view improves your spatial resolution.
• Increasing x-rays will NOT improve Maximum spatial resolution
Hat Panel Detectors (DR): These can be direct (x-ray to signal) or indirect (x-ray to light to signal)
• This v.v That: Indirect uses a scintillator. Direct uses a photoconductor
• This vs That: Indirect uses Cesium Iodine (a scintillator). Direct uses Amorphous Selenium, (the
photoconductor)
• This vs That: Both Indirect and Direct use a TFT read out array
• Spatial Resolution for DR is Superior to CR because the pixel detector is built into the DR flat panel -
• Direct systems that avoid lateral dispersion of light have better spatial resolution
• Spatial Resolution for Flat Panel Detectors is limited to the DEL(detector element); smaller detector
elements = better spatial resolution.
