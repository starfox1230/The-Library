PET QA
Just like there is QA on all the other pieces of equipment in nuclear medicine, PET also has a
whole bunch of QA. I've chosen two QA tests which I think are the most high yield. 1 believe
they arc high yield because their names sound similar and that means they'd make good
distraetors for each other on multiple choice.
Blank Scan - DAILY - It’s best to think about this as the PEI' equivalent of the uniformity scan.
The purpose of the scan is to help keep the attenuation correction data accurate. This is done with
nothing in the FOV. You simply use the systems transmission radiation source. I think about this
as “ zeroing” the scanner, or setting it as a “ blank slate." Remember - it's done daily (you want to
start each day with a blank slate).
Normalization Scan - MONTI ILY - This corrects for discrepancies in the thousands of detector
elements. You scan a calibrated position source placed in the FOV. The scan serves to
“ normalize” the detection lines. If your “ normalization” is fucked up you will see horizontal
linear streaks in the images, and nothing is “ normal” about having horizontal linear streaks. This
should normally be done once a month... to keep things normal. (“ N-M” Normal Month).

power loss can imbalance the bucket setup / tube balancing. This manifests as a dark block
rotating on the sinogram.
