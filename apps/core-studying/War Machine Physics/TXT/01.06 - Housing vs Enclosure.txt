Anode - This is the positively charged target. It seduces the electron with it’s
positive charge. Typically it’s made of Tungsten.
Why Tungsten?
1) It can tolerate a lot of heat.
2) Because of it’s high Z. X-ray yield is proportional to Z squared.
Excitation - This occurs when you have energy transfer from the incident particle to an orbital
electron in the target. The transfer moves the electron to a high energy state, but not high enough
to actually remove it from the shell. When it finally calms down (returns to a lower energy state)
there is emission of heat - but no x-ray production.
Ionization - If the energy of the incident particle is enough to eject an electron then you have
ionization.
