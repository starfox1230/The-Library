The goal is the smallest focal spot possible (best resolution) - the trade off is heat tolerance.
“ Line Focus Principal" - Here is the general idea.... You want a small effective focal spot
(the spot on the patient). This is one of the rare situations where bigger is not better. The
smaller the better (that's not what she said) - because that gives better spatial resolution. You
might think the way to achieve this would be to tighten up you actual focal spot (the spot
where the electrons hit the target) to then make a smaller effective focal spot. 1 his would
actually work. The problem is you would burn up your target. You have to spread the pain
around. That’s where this idea of the “ Line Focus Principal” comes in. Someone - smarter
than me (and probably you) figured out that if you angle the target that it will keep your large
actual focal spot (for the purpose of dispersing heat) but narrow up your effective focal spot.
