The term unethical means doing something that is wrong or unfain even if it’s not against the

Greek and Latin roots: Extort comes from the Latin word extorquere which means to get

Now this term was being used to describe some of the wealthy tycoons of the time who did the

A monopoly is when a single company or group controls an entire industry or market, with no
rivals. This means they have the power to set prices and make decisions based only on what is
best for their company. Because there's no competition, they can often charge higher prices
and offer fewer choices to consumers.

Let's pop into the present day to understand what a monopoly is:

Before, you could choose from different phone models. Maybe you wanted one with a great
camera or one that folds. But now, with Talkit in charge, you have no options. You have to buy
their phone, no matter what you want and at whatever price they decide.

Monopolies are not good for consumers (the people like you and me who buy things). They offen

If you play Monopoly, you can pretend to be a robber baron tycoon and create your own
monopolies.

Monopoly is a board game that mirrors the concept of monopolies in real life. Here's how it does

Players buy properties on the board. As you acquire (buy) more properties, you can charge
higher rents. This mirrors how real-life companies buy up competition and gain control over an
entire market.

When you own all properties in a color group, you have a monopoly. This allows you to build
houses and hotels, drastically increasing the rent you can charge. In real life, monopolies can
set higher prices because they have no competition.

As one player gains more properties and money, they start to dominate the game. Other
players struggle to compete and often end up bankrupt. This represents how monopolies can
push competitors out of the market and control the economy.

Imagine being so rich that you could throw an extravagant party that costs as much as buying
several houses! That is what many tycoons did on a regular basis. At some events, there were
feasts with hundreds of dishes, fountains flowing with champagne, and live performances by
famous musicians. Guests often received expensive pieces of jewelry, like diamond brooches or
gold watches, as party favors.

Many tycoons had their own private trains and luxurious yachts. These were like moving
mansions, with fancy furniture, bedrooms, dining areas, and even servants.

While most people in the world rarely traveled far from home, these wealthy individuals toured
the globe, staying in the best hotels and visiting exotic locations.

The lives of the tycoons showed just how much wealth they had, but their fortunes also
highlighted the big gap between the rich and the poor during that time. While they enjoyed
enormous luxury, many workers who helped build their empires lived in very tough conditions.

As new jobs sprang up in cities across the country, many people moved to where new work was
available in textile and steel mills, slaughterhouses (where animals were processed for meat),
manufacturing plants, and on the railroads. While industries grew rapidly and created immense
fortunes for some, families hoped to claim their share of the burgeoning and newfound wealth.

Root words: The word burgeoning comes from the Old French word bor joner meaning fo bud or
sprout. It means to grow or increase rapidly. It can also mean to blossom and thrive.

Many families packed their belongings and moved to the rapidly growing cities. As they arrived,
the streets became more crowded and full of life, completely changing the look and feel of the
nation's urban areas. By 1900, an incredible 30 million people-nearly half of the U.S.
population of 76 million— were living in cities.
