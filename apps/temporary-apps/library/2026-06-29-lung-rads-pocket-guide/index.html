    Edge Cases

    Growth

    Growth is more than 1.5 mm mean diameter within a 12-month interval. If a nodule crosses a higher size threshold, classify by the new size even if formal growth is not met.

    Slow Growth

    Slow-growing non-solid GGNs can remain category 2 until they meet another criterion. Slow-growing solid or part-solid nodules are suspicious and may be category 4B.

    Infectious / Inflammatory

    Use category 0 with 1-3 month LDCT when the pattern is indeterminate for infection or inflammation, such as segmental/lobar consolidation, more than six new nodules, or a large new solid nodule appearing quickly.

    Airway

    Subsegmental airway nodules are usually category 2. Segmental or more proximal airway nodules start as 4A if suspicious at baseline, and become 4B when persistent or growing on 3-month follow-up.

    Atypical Cysts

    Thin-walled cysts are not classified. A growing cystic component in a thick-walled cyst is category 3. Thick-walled or multilocular cysts are 4A at baseline; wall/nodular/opacity progression is 4B.

    Modifier S

    Add S to categories 0-4 for clinically significant non-lung-cancer findings that need attention. Known findings already being evaluated generally do not need S unless there is concerning change.
