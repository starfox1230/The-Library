Unit Pattern

Activity
Use Ci/mCi/uCi or Bq/MBq/GBq for how much radioactive material is present.
Dose Equivalent
Use rem/mrem or Sv/mSv for biologic risk, dose limits, public release, and reporting.
Absorbed Dose
Use rad or Gy for energy deposited in tissue, deterministic effects, and ARS thresholds.
Exposure Rate
Use mR/hr or mrem/hr for measured external fields around packages, patients, rooms, and sources.
Ci, mCi, uCi, Bq, MBq, GBq

Radioactivity

These describe the source strength or administered activity. Think generators, patient-release activity tables, sewer disposal limits, leak tests, and breakthrough contamination.

Anchor: 1 mCi = 37 MBq. 0.005 uCi = 185 Bq.

rem, mrem, Sv, mSv

Dose Equivalent / Effective Dose

These are the regulatory safety units for people: occupational limits, public limits, embryo/fetus limits, breastfeeding instructions, and reportable medical events.

Anchor: 1 rem = 10 mSv. 100 mrem = 1 mSv. 0.5 rem = 5 mSv.

rad, Gy

Absorbed Dose

These show tissue energy deposition. Use them for deterministic effects, very high radiation areas, ARS, LD values in Gy, and skin/lens thresholds.

Anchor: 100 rad = 1 Gy. Very high radiation area: 500 rad (5 Gy) in 1 hour at 1 m.

mrem/hr, mSv/hr, distance

External Fields

Use rate plus distance for package labels, room surveys, patient discharge readings, and radiation-area postings.

Anchor: unrestricted area is less than 2 mrem in any 1 hour; I-131 discharge is often 7 mrem/hr at 1 m.
